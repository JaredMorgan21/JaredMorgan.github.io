Trajectory Optimization through Mixed-Integer Optimization of Contact Dynamics
My master's thesis. This work explores solving legged robot trajectories where the selection of end effector dynamics are optimizable. This problem lends itself well to mixed-integer optimization, an optimization framework that combines linear inputs and states such as forces and positions with integer or binary decisions such as contact or contact dynamics. This work presents a novel contribution to the world of legged robotics and increases their decision-making capacity. Submitted to ICRA 2026 and redacted until further notice.
PACE: Perception-aware Contact Estimation and Slip Detection through Dynamic Map Analysis
Work continued from my senior capstone. This work presents a novel framework for adjusting the weighting of sources of contact detection according to terrain information. Specialized for stairs, this work improved our ability to detect loss of contact around large discrete changes in height where accurate state estimation is highly important for recovery. Submitted to ICRA 2026 and redacted until further notice.

Automated Testing and Sorting of PCBs
Automated the testing and sorting of PCBs using a UR5. I used ethernet to communicate between the central control software, the test software, and the UR5. Used sensors to catalogue PCBs, track their pass/fail in inventory, and make decisions concerning robot motion. Custom milled PCB grippers to allow arm tolerance by sliding PCBs into desired location.
Quadrotor Control to Intercept UAVs
This work represents the culmination of my first modern controls course. Here we linearized the dynamics of the highly nonlinear quadrotor and implemented a LQR solution with the addition of integral action. This allowed us to solve the problem of intercepting a UAV within a controlled airspace and bringing it back to home base while increasing efforts as the UAV resisted. The problem? Not all small-angle approximations are equally valid and the instability of linearizing cosine caused an imbalance in accepted forces in one direction. This project kicked off my love of modern controls and desire to research increasingly nonlinear problems.
Robot Manipulator Control and Sorting
Sorted and tracked objects by color and shape using OpenCV. Converted detected pixels into real-world positions and generated trajectories with associated joint torques to allow a 3-dof robot arm to pick and place the objects.
Robot Maze Exploration

WPI Affirmations Generator
8 months before the AI boom created by the release of ChatGPT, I employed Tensorflow to train a RNN-based NLG model to generate text based on a database of affirmations submitted to a popular meme page on campus. Combined with a pipeline for sourcing and editing images, I and the team I worked with created a program that automatically generated memes specific to our campus. I'm not saying OpenAI was inspired by this project, but I am saying GPT-4 was released afterwards.
Large Object Approximation for Robot Swarms
Using simple controls principles and a lot of geometry, I developed a method for decentralized swarms to surround and collectively estimate the center of an object, represented here by a herd of sheep.
Reinforcement Learning for Aircraft Control
Completed as a final project for my reinforcement learning class, this project explores the use of DDPG and PPO for an aircraft control problem. The aircraft must exit a controlled airspace while navigating obstacles, avoiding other aircraft, and controlling its altitude.
